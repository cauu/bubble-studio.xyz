## Introduction

As we age, our bodies naturally decline—that's just how life works. But scientific research tells us: **the rate of aging can be controlled**.

Take two 70-year-olds: one might be physically strong and mentally sharp, while the other struggles with chronic illness. The difference largely comes down to daily habits.

This guide avoids complex medical jargon. Instead, it focuses on "what to do" and "why it works".

---

## 1. Diet: What to Eat and How

### What to Eat More Of

**Fish and Chicken**
- Eat fish 2-3 times per week, especially fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
- Chicken without skin is healthier than red meat (beef, pork, lamb)
- Research proves that people who eat white meat live longer

**Vegetables and Fruits**
- Half a pound of vegetables daily (after cooking)
- One apple or banana worth of fruit each day
- More colors are better: green leafy vegetables, red tomatoes, purple eggplant
- Nutrients in vegetables and fruits can reduce mortality risk by 20%

**Nuts**
- A small handful daily (about 30g)
- Walnuts, peanuts, almonds all work
- People who eat nuts 5 times per week have 25% lower cardiovascular mortality

**Tea and Coffee**
- Green tea, black tea—2-3 cups daily
- Coffee is fine too, but no more than 2 cups
- Tea drinkers live more than 1 year longer on average

### What to Eat Less Of

**Red Meat**
- Beef, pork, lamb—no more than twice per week
- Especially limit fatty cuts and processed meats (sausages, bacon)

**Sugary Drinks**
- Soda, bubble tea, fruit juices all count
- Water and light tea are best
- One sugary drink per day increases mortality risk by 7%

**Processed Foods**
- Canned goods, instant noodles, hot dogs
- These foods contain too much salt and additives

### How to Eat

**Control Eating Times**
- Don't eat within 1 hour of waking up (water is fine)
- Finish dinner 3 hours before bedtime

**Eating Order**
- Vegetables first, then protein, then carbohydrates
- This keeps blood sugar more stable

## 2. Exercise: Never Too Late to Start

### What's the Best Exercise?

**Racket Sports Are Most Effective**
- Table tennis, badminton, tennis
- 2-3 times per week, about 45 minutes each
- Studies show racket sports reduce all-cause mortality by 47%

**Walking Works Too**
- 6,000-8,000 steps daily
- Don't need to do it all at once
- Walking can reduce mortality risk by over 50%

**Strength Training Matters**
- Lift water bottles
- Do squats while holding a chair
- 2-3 times per week, 10-15 minutes each
- Muscle loss is one of the biggest health threats for seniors

### How to Gauge Intensity?

Simple test:
- When walking or exercising, you should be able to speak normally but not sing—that's the right intensity
- If you're too breathless to speak, slow down

### Safety Tips

- Those with high blood pressure or heart conditions should consult a doctor first
- Warm up for 5 minutes before exercising (swing arms, rotate waist)
- Don't sit down immediately after exercising—walk slowly to cool down
- Stop immediately if you feel unwell

## 3. Sleep: Good Sleep, Good Health

### How Much Sleep?

- **7 hours** per night is optimal
- Too little (under 6 hours) or too much (over 9 hours) both have risks

### When to Sleep?

- Best to go to bed between 10-11 PM
- Wake up between 6-7 AM
- Try to maintain the same schedule every day

### How to Sleep Better?

**Morning Sunlight**
- Stand on your balcony or in your yard for 5-10 minutes after waking
- Sunlight helps regulate your biological clock

**Limit Screen Time at Night**
- Avoid phones and TV for 1 hour before bed
- If you must use screens, dim them and enable night mode

**Foot Soak Before Bed**
- Soak feet in warm water (about 40°C/104°F) for 15 minutes
- A warm body falls asleep more easily

**Relaxation**
- Lie in bed, breathe in slowly, breathe out slowly
- Repeat several times to help relax

## 4. Mental Health: Better Mood, Fewer Health Problems

### 1. Stay Social

- Chat with family daily
- Call or meet with old friends
- Join community activities
- Lonely people have 26% higher mortality risk—equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes per day

### 2. Stay Curious

- Learn something new: gardening, calligraphy, using a smartphone
- Read books and newspapers
- New experiences protect the brain

### 3. Manage Stress

- Don't get too worked up over problems
- Try deep breathing: inhale 4 seconds, hold 4 seconds, exhale 4 seconds
- Repeat several times to calm down

## 5. Essential Health Screenings

### Annual Basic Check-ups

**Blood Tests**: Blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol, liver and kidney function

**Heart Check**: ECG

**Cancer Screening**:
- Older men: Prostate exam
- Older women: Breast exam, cervical exam
- Over 50: Colonoscopy (every 5 years)

**Others**: Bone density scan (especially for women), eye exam

---

## 6. Daily Checklist

### Morning

1. Get 5-10 minutes of sunlight on your balcony
2. Wash your face with cool water (not ice cold)
3. Drink a glass of water

### Daytime

4. Walk 6,000+ steps
5. Eat enough vegetables
6. Talk to someone

### Evening

7. Soak feet or take a warm bath
8. Put down your phone
9. Do some deep breathing

---

## 7. Things to Avoid

### Absolutely Don't Do

- **Smoking**: Reduces life expectancy by over 10 years
- **Heavy drinking**: No amount of alcohol is safe

### Minimize

- **Sitting too long**: Stand up and move for 5 minutes every hour
- **Staying up late**: Try to be in bed by 11 PM
- **Eating leftovers**: Especially overnight leafy greens

---

## Summary in One Sentence

**Four things to do for better health:**

1. **Eat right**: More fish and vegetables, less fatty meat and sugar
2. **Move**: Walk 6,000+ steps daily
3. **Sleep well**: Get 7 hours every night
4. **Stay connected**: Talk more with family and friends

## Important Note

- This guide provides lifestyle advice for healthy seniors
- If you have chronic conditions, please consult your doctor first
- Seek medical attention promptly when feeling unwell—don't tough it out
